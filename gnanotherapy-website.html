Redefining Immune-Mediated Disease Treatment

Moving beyond broadly suppressive agents to precision-targeted therapies that restore immune homeostasis and achieve sustained remission.

Our Differentiated Approach

Current autoimmune treatments suppress both pathogenic and protective immune cells, leading to serious adverse effects while rarely achieving cure. Our nanoparticle platform selectively targets and reprograms disease-causing cells while preserving protective immunity.

Through localized delivery of growth factors directly to lymphoid tissues, we achieve therapeutic concentrations 100-1000 times lower than systemic administration, dramatically reducing side effects and treatment costs while enhancing efficacy.

Targeted Delivery

Nanoparticles attach to specific T cells in lymphoid clusters, delivering therapeutic agents precisely where needed.

Immune Restoration

Induces regulatory T cell proliferation and differentiation to restore natural immune balance and tolerance.

Sustained Remission

Achieves long-term disease control through immune system reprogramming rather than continuous suppression.

Advanced Nanoparticle Platform Technology

A novel therapeutic approach that functions as artificial antigen-presenting cells (aAPC) to induce and expand multiple populations of suppressive regulatory cells in vivo.

Key Platform Capabilities

  • Expands CD4+, CD8+ T cells and NK cells for comprehensive immune regulation
  • Delivers essential stimulation and cytokines for regulatory cell generation
  • Achieves therapeutic effects without toxic immunosuppressive agents
  • Reduces systemic growth factor exposure by 100-1000 fold
  • Applicable to autoimmune diseases, GVHD, and transplant rejection

Precision Targeting

Controlled Release

Scientific Leadership

David Horwitz, MD

Scientific Founder

Internationally recognized physician-scientist specializing in T cell biology and immune regulation. Dr. Horwitz characterized key abnormalities in systemic lupus erythematosus and was first to identify the role of IL-2 and TGF-Î² in regulatory T cell development.

His pioneering work in Treg biology led to the development of our targeted nanoparticle approach, which repairs defective immune regulation through localized delivery of growth factors to lymphoid tissues. This breakthrough reduces systemic exposure by 100-1000 fold while dramatically improving therapeutic outcomes.

Recently developed two novel nanoformulations that induce multiple therapeutic immune cell populations using only IL-2, with target cells producing required TGF-Î² endogenously, further minimizing potential adverse effects.

Tarek Fahmy, PhD

Scientific Co-Founder

Associate Professor of Biomedical Engineering at Yale University and internationally recognized leader in nanoimmunology. Dr. Fahmy's laboratory focuses on developing biomaterials for immune system modulation and has produced over 20 patents in engineered materials.

Beginning his career as a research engineer at DuPont, Dr. Fahmy transitioned to biomedical applications, bringing unique expertise in materials science to immunotherapy development.

Previously co-founded Toralgen with Dr. Horwitz and Gerald Rea to develop oral bile acid nanoparticle platforms for diabetes treatment. General Nanotherapeutics was established to advance targeted nanoparticle therapies for broader immune-mediated indications.
